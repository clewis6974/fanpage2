The Life of Frank Sinatra

Page by Camden Lewis

Biography

Frank Sinatra, born in Hoboken New York, on December 12, 1915. Is who would eventually become the face of jazz music across the United States. As a young child in New York, his parents ran a tavern where he would sing for a little extra cash. He loved to listen to the radio and try to emulate his idol, Bing Crosby. He continued to keep singing despite what his parents tried to get him to do. While they did recognize his talents, they tried to get Sinatra to follow a more "traditional" career. When he got to high school, Sinatra was expelled for general roudiness. This is what began his reputation as a bad-boy. Later on, Sinatra got arrested for adultery but was soon after let out. By 1935, he got his first big break as a lead singer with a local quartet named the Hoboken Four. After this, he started working at a roadhouse in New Jersey while only making $15 per week. The benefit to this was that the roadhouse was connected to the WNEW radio station in New York City and began performing with a group live during the Dance Parade Show. Then after this he split to be solo. He released his debut album "DayBreak" that featured Tommy Dorsey in October of 1940. This album is what boosted his career to become the king of jazz.

The Rat Pack

The Rat Pack were a group of artists that all had similar backgrounds and singing styles. Sinatra was a part of this group. The others were men named Dean Martin, Peter Lawford, Joey Bishop, and Sammy Davis Jr.. They were basically just a group of guys that had multiple things in common and made music together.
